IMF's New Working Paper on Residential Real Estate in China

By Wildsheepchase, 2015 06 03 Wednesday, in category 2015

Economy, Housing, ReadingNotes

IMF recently published a very good report on China's residential real estate sector. It's a cliche to talk about China's housing "bubble"" without putting some numbers around it.

Here's the link to the report.

My main takeaways:

1) The four largest cities (tier 1) only account for 10% of floor space sold. Tier 2 cities (usually provincial capitals) account for about 50%, and smaller cities account for more than 40%. So even thought most investors tend to focus on property prices in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou and Shenzhen, they are simply not that important to the overall investments growth of the country.

2) NBS data suggest that the nationwide housing inventory ratio to be only about four months of sales. FangGuanJu's data, which is probably more comprehensive and less subject to property developers' mis-reporting, suggest that the nationwide ratio might have been as high as 24 months in mid-2014!

3) Housing inventory overhang is mostly a tier 2 and 3/4 cities phenomenon. We shouldn't be too worried about property prices in tier 1 cities.

4) This round of housing downturn appears to be much more severe than previous ones. With local governments running out of funds from selling land, it is not surprising that fiscal spendings have fallen off the cliff since 2014Q4, which has been the main culprit for recent weaknesses in the economy.

5) China's per-capital housing space is already on par with developed economies, and much higher than other Asian countries with high population density. The housing construction story should be behind us. I made this point while still working as an economist/strategist back in 2010. It's good to see more detailed data to back this idea.
